Upgrading Warm Neutral to Premium

Same neutral palette, but every detail refined. Here's what separates "cheap" from "expensive" in dark UI design.

The 7 things that separate cheap from premium

1. Buttons — padding ratio, weight, radius

Cheap buttons are too tall, too bold, too rounded. Premium buttons have generous horizontal padding, medium weight, and subtle radius.

Premium UIs use tighter letter-spacing on headings, looser on labels, and much more contrast between heading and body weight.

What changed vs. the "cheap" version

Buttons

Wider horizontal padding (px-6), shorter height (py-2), tighter radius (rounded-md), inner highlight shadow, tinted CTA color instead of pure white

Typography

Negative letter-spacing on large numbers/headings, small-caps labels with wide tracking, 13px body text (not 12 or 14), font-semibold not font-bold

Cards

Ring shadow (0 0 0 1px) instead of border, subtle top-edge highlight gradient, more internal padding, warm/cool-tinted background

Surfaces

Never pure gray — either warm (#161615) or cool (#151618) tint. 3 levels max. Dividers at 3-4% opacity, not 6-8%

Hover states

bg-white/[0.04] max — barely perceptible. transition-colors only (not transition-all). Text shifts from gray-500 to gray-300, never to white

Spacing

More generous top-level padding (py-10, px-8). Tighter internal gaps (gap-3 not gap-4). Asymmetric padding where appropriate

Pick F1 (Warm Cream), F2 (Cool Silver), or F3 (Editorial Bone) — or mix elements.
